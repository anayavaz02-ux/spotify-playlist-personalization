# 🎧 How Listening Behavior Shapes Playlists  
## A Data Analytics Approach to Music Personalization

---

## 🧠 The Question

Have you ever opened Spotify and felt like the playlist was made just for you?

Whether it’s a morning mix or a late-night playlist, the recommendations often feel intuitive—almost like the platform understands your mood.

So what’s really happening?

> Is it complex AI… or just smart use of data?

---

## 🎯 Project Objective

This project simulates how music platforms personalize playlists using **user listening behavior**.

It focuses on answering:

- How can we identify what a user likes?  
- Do preferences change throughout the day?  
- How can we turn behavior into curated playlists?  

---

## 🧩 Approach

This project recreates the core logic behind playlist personalization in a simple, data-driven way:

### 1. Simulating Listening Behavior
A dataset was generated to represent:
- User listening activity  
- Song genres  
- Time of listening (Morning, Afternoon, Evening, Night)  
- Frequency of listens  

---

### 2. Identifying Preferences
User preferences were derived by analyzing:
- Genre-wise listening frequency  
- Repeated engagement patterns  

👉 The most listened genre is treated as the user’s primary preference  

---

### 3. Adding Context (Time-Based Behavior)
Listening patterns were analyzed across different times of day to capture **contextual preferences**

---

### 4. Generating Curated Playlists
Playlists were created by combining:
- User’s preferred genres  
- Time-of-day behavior  
- High-frequency listening signals  

---

## 📊 Key Insights

### 🔹 Pop as a Universal Anchor
Pop consistently dominates listening across all time periods, peaking in the evening.

👉 **Implication:**  
Pop acts as a “safe” genre that can be included in most playlists to maintain familiarity.

---

### 🔹 Evening = Peak Engagement
Listening activity is highest in the evening across multiple genres.

👉 **Implication:**  
This is the ideal window for diverse and high-engagement playlists.

---

### 🔹 Time Shapes Taste
- Hip-Hop increases toward night  
- Morning listening is more balanced  

👉 **Implication:**  
User preferences are not static—they depend on context.

---

### 🔹 Niche vs Dominant Genres
- EDM shows consistently low engagement  
- Indie remains stable but moderate  

👉 **Implication:**  
Not all genres should be treated equally in recommendations.

---

## 🔁 How Playlists Are Actually Curated

This project highlights a key idea:

> Personalization is not about predicting everything—it’s about reinforcing patterns.

Curated playlists are built using:
- **Dominance** → What users listen to most  
- **Consistency** → What they repeat  
- **Context** → When they listen
